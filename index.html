Kepler's laws of planetary motion : The second Law

  • Explanatory Video
  • History
  • Statement

History

In the early 17th century, Johannes Kepler, a German mathematician and astronomer came up with three laws of planetary motion after analysing astronomical data collected by an astronomer named Tycho Brahe. These laws are called Kepler's laws of planetary motion and they served as a grand milestone in estabilishing the Heliocentric model of the solar system. In this video we will be discussing about the second law in specific.

Statement

The second law is also known as the law of equal areas. According to the second law “A line joining a planet and the Sun sweeps out equal areas during equal intervals of time. “
